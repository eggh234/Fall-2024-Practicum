When the pepper spray makes contact, a sharp burning sensation spreads across the eyes, making it nearly impossible to keep them open. As the irritation intensifies, the chemicals inside cause your eyelids to swell. Within seconds, it can lead to temporary blindness, making it difficult to see. And as you breathe, the chemicals can get into your throat, causing it to tighten. These symptoms can last up to an hour, until the effects gradually wear off.
